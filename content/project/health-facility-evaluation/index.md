## Overview

As part of the **Health System Strengthening** initiative under the Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS), Bangladesh — in collaboration with the World Health Organization — this project developed and deployed a comprehensive national evaluation framework across 450+ public and private healthcare facilities.

## Key Activities

- **Facility Evaluation Framework Design** — Directed the development of a multi-dimensional performance evaluation tool integrating patient-reported experience measures (PREMs), SDG indicators, and 100 WHO core health indicators with routine DHIS2 data
- **Tool Development & Validation** — Designed, piloted, and validated qualitative and quantitative survey instruments for health facility performance assessment
- **National Rollout** — Coordinated data collection, field supervision, and quality assurance across 450+ facilities spanning multiple districts
- **Capacity Building** — Trained physicians and health facility staff on evaluation methodology, reporting protocols, and DHIS2 data management
- **National Health Bulletin** — Served as Managing Editor of the National Health Bulletin 2017, compiling and presenting national health performance data for policy use

## Impact

Established a replicable, WHO-endorsed performance evaluation methodology for Bangladesh's national health system, informing resource allocation, quality improvement priorities, and Health System Strengthening strategy.

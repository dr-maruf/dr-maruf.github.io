## Overview

This WHO-supported initiative developed structured patient safety and service quality measurement tools for deployment across government and private healthcare facilities, with the goal of strengthening community-based care quality and accountability in Bangladesh's primary and sub-district health system.

## Key Activities

- **Tool Development** — Designed and validated patient safety and service quality assessment instruments adaptable to both government and private facility contexts
- **SOP Development** — Developed standard operating procedures for patient participation, informed consent, and ethical oversight in clinical data collection and research activities
- **Field Implementation** — Supervised field deployment, ensuring consistency in data collection methodology across diverse facility types and geographic contexts
- **Data Analysis & Reporting** — Analysed facility-level data, prepared summary reports, and formulated improvement recommendations for programme management
- **Stakeholder Training** — Trained facility staff and district health managers on tool application, data recording, and quality improvement action planning

## Impact

Contributed to measurable improvements in care accountability and patient experience reporting at the sub-district level, building local capacity for ongoing service quality monitoring within the national health system.
